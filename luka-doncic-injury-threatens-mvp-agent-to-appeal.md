# Luka Dončić Injury Threatens MVP: Agent to Appeal

Los Angeles Lakers star **Luka Dončić** has suffered a **Grade 2 left hamstring strain** that is expected to keep him out for the remainder of the 2025‑26 NBA regular season, putting his bid for the **Most Valuable Player (MVP)** award in jeopardy. Dončić had been leading the league in scoring and was among the top contenders for MVP before the injury sidelined him with only **64 games played** — one shy of the NBA’s **65‑game minimum requirement** for award eligibility.

Despite the setback, Dončić’s agent, **Bill Duffy of WME Basketball**, announced plans to file an **“Extraordinary Circumstances Challenge”** with the NBA. This grievance is designed to allow players to be eligible for awards even if they fall short of the minimum games played due to circumstances beyond their control. Duffy pointed out that Dončić had previously missed two games earlier in the season to attend the birth of his daughter overseas, suggesting that this qualifies as an extraordinary circumstance.

“This season, Luka Dončić has performed at a historic level, leading the league in scoring, carrying the Lakers to third place in the Western Conference, and placing himself in the middle of one of the most tightly contested MVP races in memory,” Duffy said in a statement. He added that he hopes the league will recognize Dončić’s accomplishments and grant eligibility under the rule exception.

The Lakers have already confirmed that Dončić will miss at least the final five regular‑season games. With the playoffs approaching soon, his availability for postseason games remains uncertain. The injury and the eligibility challenge have sparked debate among fans and analysts about how award rules should be applied in rare situations like this.
